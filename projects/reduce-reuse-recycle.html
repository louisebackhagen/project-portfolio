Reduce, Reuse and Recycle

2019 User Research Interactive Prototyping Adobe XD

Course: Media Lab.

Challenge

The project explored how to encourage more positive and sustainable consumption behavior in everyday life.

Complex Decisions: People often struggle with where to recycle and how to sort products made of multiple materials.

Daily Friction: Home waste storage and perceived effort make recycling harder to maintain.

Motivation Gap: Even users with good intent can lose motivation when the process feels slow.

My Role

I contributed to user research, synthesis, and interaction design. I helped map user pain points, define feature priorities, and design an interactive prototype in Adobe XD focused on practical recycling support.

Process

Research: We conducted literature review and market scanning to understand existing sustainability and recycling challenges.

Exploration: We created low-fidelity concepts to test early ideas and narrow down the most useful interaction patterns.

Data Collection: A survey captured current recycling behaviors and barriers, with 55 responses forming the basis for analysis.

Synthesis: We used affinity mapping to cluster findings and identify recurring behavioral and usability problems.

Prototype: We refined the concept into a high-fidelity Adobe XD prototype aligned with the validated insights.

Delivery & Impact

Structured Support: The concept translated broad sustainability goals into practical, day-to-day recycling guidance.

Core Feature Set: The final prototype included Explore, Profile, Station Locator, Scanner, and Product Page.

User-Centered Direction: The solution direction was grounded in observed behavior and survey data rather than assumptions.

Scalable Concept: The design created a foundation that can be extended with more personalized nudges and recycling guidance over time.
